Assignment 2B: One scatter plot and two datasets

Description

We have created a scatter plot that visualize 'Prostitution' and 'Vehicle Theft' crimes count within different San Fransisco districts.
Each point in the graph correspond to a district (like labeled) with an X-value defined by that district's prostitution crime count and an Y-value corresponding to the vehicle theft crime count for that district.
Furthermore, the radius of a given point is given by the total amount of crimes (prostitution + vehicle theft) in that district
One can change the year (2003 or 2015) you want to investigate, by clicking anywhere on the graph.

Explain in your own words why you think I want the axis to be the same for both years?
To get a clear view of the development of the relationship between the crimes, we want the axes to be the same, so we don't distort the visual representation.

Assignment 2C: One barchart and (at least) two datasets

Description

We have created a histogram that investigates a specific crime by looking at what time the crime occur.
The data comes from San Fransiscos districts, from the year 2003 to 2016.
Each bar inside our histogram has a height of the total amount of crimes (like labeled) in that crime type, which are inside a specific time interval.
The specific time interval is along the X-axis and it should be interpreted as 0 correspond to the interval from 0 to 1 hour, 1 is between 1-2, ..., 23 is 23-24.
To make the visualization complete, we have added a differentation in the color such that the lower the total amount of crimes, the darker the color.
We have made the possibility to change between different crime types by clicking on the graph. The transistion between the different graphs is made smooth to please the eye.

Assignment 2D: Visualizing geodata

Description

The last diagram is a K-Means clustering visualization. The K-Means algorithm is trying to find groups of data in a dataset which are close together (aka. clusters).
The data we are working with here is every case of prostitution in SF in the years 2003 to 2016 (What has been recorded so far).
Each cluster in the K-means, is colored so you can see the clusters as we increase the amount of clusters.
We have also added the center of each cluster. The cluster-center is the center point of the circles with the black outlining.
To change the amount of clusters, simply click the map, and it will cycle from 2-10.
